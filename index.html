Digital Signature

A digital signature is an electronic stamp of authentication on digital information, like emails or documents. It uses cryptography to verify the authenticity and integrity of the information, ensuring it originated from the signer and hasn't been altered. Think of it as a digital equivalent to a handwritten signature or stamped seal, but with built-in security.
